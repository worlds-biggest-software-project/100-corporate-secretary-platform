# Corporate Secretary Platform

> Part of the [worlds-biggest-software-project](https://github.com/worlds-biggest-software-project) initiative.
>
> An AI-native, open-source corporate secretary platform that unifies board meeting management, minutes, resolutions, and multi-entity governance.

The Corporate Secretary Platform combines a secure board portal with native entity and subsidiary management, AI-drafted minutes, and cross-jurisdictional compliance tracking. It targets general counsel, company secretaries, board chairs, and finance leaders at mid-market companies, private-equity portfolio groups, and nonprofits — buyers currently caught between accessible-but-shallow SME tools and very expensive enterprise GRC suites.

---

## Why Corporate Secretary Platform?

- Enterprise platforms such as Diligent One and Nasdaq Boardvantage typically cost USD 20,000–150,000+ per year, pricing mid-market companies and nonprofits out of integrated governance tooling.
- SME-friendly tools (BoardPro, ContractZen) are affordable but lack entity management depth, multi-jurisdictional compliance, and serious AI capabilities.
- Entity management and the board portal are usually sold as separate modules even within a single vendor's suite, forcing cross-module configuration and duplicate data entry.
- Legacy entity systems like GEMS / CT Corporation have aging UX and minimal AI investment, despite being deeply embedded in Fortune 500 legal departments.
- No meaningful open-source corporate secretary or board portal platform exists today, leaving the entire category dependent on proprietary SaaS with custom enterprise pricing.

## Key Features

### Board Meeting Lifecycle

- Secure board book assembly, distribution, and version-controlled document library with granular role-based access
- Agenda builder with templates, time allocation, and committee-level structures
- Digital minute-taking with action item extraction and attendance recording
- Presenter mode, annotations, and private director notes
- Mobile-responsive iOS and Android access with offline capability

### Resolutions, Voting, and Signatures

- E-voting and written resolutions with audit-ready records
- E-signature workflow compliant with ESIGN, UETA, and eIDAS
- Resolution and written-consent drafting from structured precedents
- Committee management with separate books, memberships, and permissions

### Entity and Subsidiary Management

- Centralised corporate record across multiple entities and subsidiaries
- Officer and director registers with appointment and resignation tracking
- Jurisdiction-specific compliance calendars and filing-deadline reminders
- Corporate structure charts and ownership visualisation
- Document vault for constitutional documents, share certificates, and resolutions

### Compliance, Audit, and Security

- Audit trail and access logging aligned with ISO 15489 and SOC 2 expectations
- ISO/IEC 27001-aligned information security posture
- GDPR / CCPA-aware handling of director PII and board materials
- Data residency options and granular per-document, per-user, per-meeting access controls
- Single sign-on via SAML, OAuth, and Active Directory

### AI-Powered Governance

- AI-drafted minutes from transcripts or notes, mapped to agenda items, decisions, and action items
- AI summarisation of lengthy board materials into pre-meeting briefs
- Resolution and consent generation from a precedent library
- Multi-jurisdiction regulatory change monitoring with entity-level impact flagging
- Governance risk scoring from board composition, attendance, and committee activity

## AI-Native Advantage

Incumbents are bolting AI onto legacy meeting workflows; an AI-native design treats agentic automation as the core. LLM-driven minute drafting, document summarisation, and resolution generation reduce hours of company-secretary work per meeting, while continuous AI agents monitor filing deadlines, director appointment expiries, and statutory register inconsistencies across hundreds of subsidiary entities. Regulatory change tracking across Companies Acts, SEC rules, and exchange listing requirements lets legal teams react before breaches occur — a capability no current vendor has fully delivered. Combined with governance risk scoring over board composition, ESG disclosures, and voting patterns, AI surfaces signals that humans miss in large document sets.

## Tech Stack & Deployment

The platform is designed for both cloud and self-hosted deployment, with data-residency options for US, EU, and APAC jurisdictions to match GDPR, Australian Privacy Act, and PDPA requirements. Integration targets include SSO providers (Okta, Azure AD, SAML 2.0), e-signature providers (DocuSign and equivalents), Microsoft Teams and Zoom for hybrid meetings, and registered-agent data feeds for automated compliance updates. Standards alignment covers XBRL / iXBRL output for SEC filers, ISO 15489 records management, ISO/IEC 27001 information security, and ESIGN / eIDAS for electronic signatures.

## Market Context

The global board management software market was valued at approximately USD 2.55 billion in 2025, projected to reach USD 5.56 billion by 2034 at a 9.22% CAGR (Market Research Future, 2024). Pricing today ranges from roughly USD 3,000–5,000 per year for SME tools up to USD 50,000–500,000+ per year for full entity-management suites, with enterprise board portals typically USD 20,000–150,000+. Primary buyers are general counsel and corporate secretaries at public companies, company-secretarial teams in private-equity portfolios, board chairs at listed companies, CFOs and COOs at mid-sized private companies, and nonprofit executive directors.
