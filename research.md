## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

| Standard | Relevance |
|----------|-----------|
| XBRL (eXtensible Business Reporting Language) v2.1 | Open standard for structured financial and regulatory data, mandated by the SEC for inline XBRL filings; relevant to regulatory reporting modules |
| iXBRL (Inline XBRL) | SEC-mandated format for annual and quarterly reports (10-K, 10-Q); board platforms that generate regulatory filings must produce compliant iXBRL output |
| ISO 15489 (Records Management) | International standard for managing organizational records; applies to the retention and disposition of board minutes, resolutions, and governance documents |
| ISO/IEC 27001 (Information Security Management) | Governance platforms holding sensitive board materials must demonstrate compliance with this information security standard |
| eIDAS (Electronic Identification, Authentication and Trust Services) | EU regulation governing qualified electronic signatures on resolutions and consents; relevant for EU-operating entities |
| ESIGN Act / UETA (USA) | US federal and state legislation validating electronic signatures on corporate resolutions and consents |
| GDPR / CCPA | Data protection regulations affecting how director PII and board documents are stored and processed |
| Corporate Governance Codes (e.g., UK Corporate Governance Code, OECD Principles) | Soft-law frameworks that define best-practice board composition, minutes, disclosure, and effectiveness reviews |

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **Automated minutes drafting:** LLMs can transcribe audio/video of board meetings and generate structured minutes with action items, decisions, and attendance records — a task currently done manually by company secretaries that takes hours per meeting.
- **Entity compliance monitoring:** AI agents can continuously monitor filing deadlines, director appointment expiries, and statutory register inconsistencies across hundreds of subsidiary entities, alerting legal teams before breaches occur.
- **Resolution and consent generation:** AI can draft board resolutions, written consents, and shareholder approvals from a structured prompt or prior precedent library, dramatically reducing legal drafting time.
- **Governance risk scoring:** By analysing board composition data, committee attendance, ESG disclosures, and shareholder voting patterns, AI can surface governance risk signals that humans would miss in large document sets.
- **Regulatory change tracking:** AI can monitor jurisdictional corporate law changes (Companies Act, SEC rules, stock exchange listing requirements) across multiple geographies and auto-flag which entities or processes require updating.
